# SQLCaseStudy_SuperstoreSales
📌 Project Overview

Retail businesses generate thousands of transactions every day, but raw sales data alone does not provide actionable insights.

In this project, I worked as a Data Analyst for a fictional retail company and transformed transactional sales data into strategic business recommendations using SQL and Business Intelligence techniques.

The goal was to identify:

✅ Revenue Drivers

✅ Profitability Opportunities

✅ Customer Purchasing Patterns

✅ Product Performance

✅ Regional Growth Opportunities

✅ Customer Retention Trends

🏢 Business Scenario

I have just joined the company's Business Intelligence team.

The executive leadership team has shared some details for company:

🔹 Sales continue to increase, but profits are inconsistent.
🔹 Some products generate high revenue but low profit.
🔹 Customer retention metrics are not being tracked.
🔹 Regional performance varies significantly.
🔹 Heavy discounting may be hurting profitability.

Management needs a data-driven strategy to support future growth.

🎯 Project Objective

The objective of this project is to analyze Superstore transactional sales data to uncover insights into customer behavior, sales performance, product effectiveness, and category profitability.

Using SQL, the analysis aims to help business stakeholders identify revenue drivers, understand customer acquisition and retention patterns, evaluate product portfolio performance, and support data-driven decision-making for future growth.

🔍 Analysis Questions
Q1. List the top 10 customers by total sales amount. Show CustomerID, full name, and total sales.

Q2. Show total sales per month for the year 2023, ordered by month.

Q3. Find out the products that have never been sold.

Q4. Find how many new customers were acquired in 2022.

Q5. Calculate the profit margin (Profit / Sales) percentage for each category.

Q6. For each category, show date-wise sales and a running total of sales over time.

Q7. Get the most recent order (by OrderDate) for every customer.

Q8. Classify customers based on their total sale, show customerid, name, totalsales.

Q9. For each category find the product with the highest totalsales.
